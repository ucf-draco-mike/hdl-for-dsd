What You Built in 4 Weeks

Week 1: Combinational + sequential fundamentals. First LED blink from HDL.

Week 2: Counters, shift registers, debounce, FSMs, hierarchy, testbenches. Verified module library.

Week 3: Memory, timing/PLL, UART TX + RX, SPI. “HELLO” on the PC terminal.

Week 4: SystemVerilog, assertions, coverage. Complete demonstrated system.

Skills You Gained

Design: Combinational, sequential, FSM, hierarchy, parameterization

Verification: Self-checking TBs, assertions, coverage awareness

Communication: UART TX/RX, SPI concepts, IP integration

Tools: Icarus, Yosys, nextpnr, GTKWave — full open-source flow

SystemVerilog: logic, always_ff/comb, enum, struct, package, assertions

Engineering practice: Incremental integration, UART debugging, timing analysis

Six categories of skills. Design, verification, communication protocols, tools, SystemVerilog, and engineering practice.

Where to Go From Here

FPGA Engineering: Larger FPGAs (Artix-7, ECP5), Vivado/Quartus, higher-speed I/O, DDR memory

ASIC Design: Standard cell flow, timing closure, DFT, physical design

Verification: UVM, constrained random, formal verification (SVA, model checking)

Hardware Security: Side-channel analysis, fault injection, logic locking, trojan detection

Embedded Systems: RISC-V soft cores, SoC integration, Linux on FPGA

Open-Source Ecosystem: Amaranth HDL, LiteX, SymbiFlow, OpenTitan

Six pathways. Tailor this to your audience — CompE students heading toward chip design, security research, embedded, or software.
